Reforming the Education - The Keen Way


by Bizploy


Posted on July 7, 2018 at 6:00 PM

Education, one major pillar in anyone’s life, is in a confused state for now. Our society, in general, invests a fair amount of capital and thought on the education sector. In a directionless manner, unfortunately, thereby yielding no noticeable change. None of us has the clarity of what is fruitful, what courses should be curated and which ones should be discarded. This investment is causing more harm than good, leading to a gradual, deep-rooted irreversible damage.


This is where, with the ongoing rapid technological advancements, data steps in. It has been floating subtly all around us since times immemorial. But little has been done to put it to constructive use. With the plethora of online courses and dedicated sites available, we already have large amounts of data accumulated.


The amalgamation of data with education ensures numerous benefits for both educational institutes as well as the students.


  1. Deeper Understanding, Better execution - Logically everything done online is data, if put to proper use could revolutionize education. With insights into student's behavioural patterns, a better approach can be mapped and iterated in order to eliminate the loopholes. This would lead the educational courses to approach the students in a relatively effective manner and vice-versa.


  2. Improved Results, Customized Programs - Currently, the only way to assess student performance and understanding is based on the answers given in tests or assignments, which is fairly superficial. In order to increase the effectiveness of educational courses offered, the data trail created by studying student's pattern of answering, skipping, reviewing questions, analysing the time taken to answer questions or study a particular section would be of enormous help. This would pave way for customized courses, better assessment of strength and weakness of every student.


  3. Reduction in Drop Rate - The education system is a funnel, wherein one thing leads to the other. Better understanding leads to customized courses which thereby leads to better results, resulting introduced dropouts. When the course is instrumental in higher success rates, it organically decreases the number of people opting out. The data tapped can be utilized for predictive analysis of the students based on their performances.


  4. Target the audience via innovative apps - An app without the right audience is a hidden masterpiece and the educational app development industry has seen a lot of innovation and there is still scope for a lot of innovation which ensures customer retention being highly interactive and innovative.

If you see, CultureAlley's app Hello English witnessed a steady rise from the month of January to may with app reach from 0.8 per cent to 1.54 per cent. Similar stats can be collected for Duolingo, from 0.12 per cent in January to 0.37 per cent in May. Within a span of five months, the kind of rise a language teaching app observed speaks volumes about the potential in the education sector and the wonders that could be done if we implement the data appropriately.


The slight slack seen is attributed to a number of reasons, majorly the examination period, which again seems to rise steeply due to the summer vacation time from April to May. The numbers are a small reflection of the massive scope which education sector has as an entity. With more data and microscopic insights, the possibility of improving increases tremendously and so does the success rates of the players in the market.

Data influx, undoubtedly, will result in better-informed decisions made by the students and the educational institutions. Without delving deep into the feasibility aspect of it, data, if utilized smartly, can entirely change the course of the education system in the years to come. It realizes what you will realize today, what you are required to have learned at this point, and what you will realize straightaway.
